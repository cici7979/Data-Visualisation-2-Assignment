Population Distribution and Living Conditions Across Malaysian States

In Malaysia, population distribution varies significantly across states, reflecting economic opportunities, geographical factors, and urbanization. Kuala Lumpur (KL), the capital city, stands as the most crowded area, with a population of over 1.7 million people packed into a small geographical space. This density is driven by KL's status as the country's main commercial, financial, and cultural hub, attracting individuals for jobs, education, and business. Other states like Selangor and Penang also have high population densities due to developed infrastructure, economic opportunities, and proximity to KL. In contrast, states like Perlis, Sabah, and Sarawak have lower populations, largely due to their rural landscapes, fewer economic opportunities, and less urbanization. This variation in population across states highlights the balance between economic development and living conditions, with urban centers being the most attractive for migration and growth.
